About Sukhothai

Sukhothai, meaning "Dawn of Happiness," was the first capital of the Kingdom of Siam and is considered the birthplace of Thai civilization. Founded in the 13th century, it played a crucial role in shaping Thailand's language, art, religion, and culture.

The city is famous for its well-preserved historical park, which features ancient temples, Buddha statues, and royal monuments surrounded by tranquil lakes and gardens. Visitors can admire remarkable architectural masterpieces that reflect the golden age of Thai history.

Today, Sukhothai Historical Park is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and remains one of Thailand's most significant cultural destinations. Its peaceful atmosphere and rich heritage offer a unique journey into the country's past.
